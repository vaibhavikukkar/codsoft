Handloom

      Handloom refers to wooden frames of different types which are used by skilled artisans to weave fabrics usually from natural fibers like Cotton, Silk, Wool, Jute etc. It is a cottage industry where the entire family is involved in the production of cloth. Right from spinning the yarn, colouring, to weaving on the loom if done by them. Fabric produced from these looms is also referred to as Handloom.
      The tools required for this entire process are made from wood, sometimes bamboo and they do not require any electricity to run them. The entire process of fabric production was totally manual in earlier times. Thus this is the most eco-friendly way of producing clothes.
      The geography of India is replete with distinct local and regional handloom traditions. Showcasing, 'Queen of sarees' & "Mahavastra of Maharashtra", the resplendent handwoven Paithani Sarees from the weaving cluster of Maharashtra.
      Nearly 2,000-year-old craft, a true significance to rich Maharashtra's culture and tradition, Paithani Sarees once produced only for the Royal families and have transcended the era of the Mughals, British, Peshwas, and still stand out aesthetically. A labor-intensive craft, splendiferous Paithani Sarees are hand woven to perfection using the finest of silks and cotton, and the process would take anything between ten days to two years, depending on the intricacy of the designs on the saree border and pallu.
      Regal and elegant, Paithani Saris have traditionally been a part of the wedding trousseau of every Maharashtrian bride. A treasured heirloom, explore Paithani Sarees from our curated collection, a must- have in every sophisticated women's handloom connoisseur's collection.
